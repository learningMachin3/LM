Thank You For Your Support

We care deeply about education so creating this podcast and community is a labor of love for us, and if you take one useful or interesting idea from our work it will all have been worth it. That being said, each episode takes 15-20 hours of work from start to finish not to mention the many other tasks involved in creating a communit beyond just the episodes. We hope to be able to continue doing this work and building this community and your support makes that possible, so thank you for being here.

The Best Way to Support Us - Join Our Patreon Community

By joining our Patreon you will gain access to exclusive community and content. We will be releasing the full-length interviews with our guests, blog posts elaborate on topics, and hosting occasional live stream round table discussions. Additionally, you gain access to our chatroom community on Discord which is where we are building a community of educators and those interested in education where you can chat with other listeners, guests, and of course us! Joining Patreon means you will be supporting us with a small monthly donation. This money enables us to pay for coffee, hosting services, and other expenses as well as allowing us to spend money and time on improving the content we are giving back to you. Most of the content we produce we are giving away completely for free, but any support you can offer is very much appreciated.

Five Ways to Help Our Community Grow

  1. Tweet at us. Let us know what you thought of an episode, and link the episode in your tweet.
  2. Join our Reddit forum where we are discussing each episode.
  3. Give us a review and rating on whatever platform you use to listen to podcasts. (This is how new listeners find us)
  4. Tell a friend about an episode you liked and share the link with them.
  5. Would you or someone you know be a great guest for us to interview? Send us an email.

Just Keep Listening

At the end of the day we created a podcast, and we put our hearts into making episodes about the uncertain future of education. The experts we interviewed are brilliant people who we think had some pretty amazing things to say. So at the end of the day, if you like our work, just keep listening.
